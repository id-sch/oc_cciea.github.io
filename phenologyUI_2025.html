Description     Three derived products (STI, TUMI and LUSI) using the UI identify the timing and strength and duration of upwelling in the CCLME (Bograd et al. 2009). The spring transition index (STI) identifies the time when upwelling starts andvaries with latitude in the CCLME. The units for STI are days and a year with a small STI value will have an earlier start to the onset of upwelling winds. The length of upwelling season index (LUSI) will provide information on the duration of upwelling during a particular year. The units for LUSI are days and a larger LUSI value indicates that the upwelling season for the given year is long. The total upwelling magnitude (TUMI) measures the ultimate amount of upwelling. There may be years of short but intense periods of upwelling, or longer but weaker upwelling seasons.

Public availability statement     Source data are publicly available.

Data sources     Bakun UI 6-hr data are downloaded from (https://oceanview.pfeg.noaa.gov/erddap/tabledap/erdUI396hr.html).

Additional Information     Bakun UI is derived from geostrophic estimates of winds derived from FNMOC sealevel pressure estimates. The 6-hr UI data are daily averaged, and cumulative UI curves are constructed for each year by taking the cumulative sum from Jan 1 to Dec 31. Details on calculating STI, LUSI, TUMI are given in (Bograd et al. 2009).

2025 Conditions

  • STI values for all three latitudes incidated an early spring transition, with 39°N having the lowest STI value below -1 sd.
  • LUSI values were all above the long-term mean indicating a longer than average upwelling season for all three latitudes.
  • TUMI values were all above the long-term mean, with the largest value near +1 sd at 33°N.
